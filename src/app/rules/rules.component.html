Chex rules

Chex is a variant of chess played on a hexagonal board. The rules are the same as in normal chess, except for the movement of the pieces.

Piece movement

King

The king can move one hexagon in any direction.
The king are blocked by other pieces.

Queen

The queen can move any number of hexagons in any direction.
The queen are blocked by other pieces.

Rook

Rooks can move any number of hexagons vertically or maximimally two hexagons in any other direction.
Rooks are blocked by other pieces.

Bishop

Bishops can move one hexagon in any direction or two hexagons not in a straight line.
Bishops are blocked by other pieces.

Knight

Knights can move two hexagons in any direction and then one hexagon with an angle of 60Â°.
Knights can jump over other pieces.

Pawn

Pawns can move one hexagon directly forward, never backward or to the side. Pawns that are further back may advance two hexagons the first time they move. Pawns can capture one hexagon diagonally forward.
When pawns reach the other side of the board, they are promoted to a queen.
Pawns are blocked by other pieces.
